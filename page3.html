Thor’s Comeback and the Final Deadlift Records

Thor’s Comeback After Boxing

After slimming down for boxing, many assumed Thor’s strength career was over. Instead, he rebuilt smart: he returned to heavy pulling while keeping conditioning and mobility. By 36, he matched—and then surpassed—his prior power.

505 kg at Eisenhart (July 2025)

On July 26, 2025 (Bavaria, Germany), Thor deadlifted 505 kg (1,113 lb) in a traditional sanctioned competition, removing lingering doubts about record context.

Watch: Thor’s 505 kg Deadlift (video)

510 kg at Giants Live (September 2025)

Eight weeks later (Sept 6, 2025, Birmingham), Thor pulled 510 kg (1,124 lb)—the heaviest deadlift ever—and still won the contest that day.

Watch (skip to 5:15): Thor’s 510 kg Pull (video)

Eddie vs Thor Deadlift Accomplishments

  • Eddie Hall: First man to deadlift 500 kg in competition (2016). Arena pressure, historic cost—but history made.
  • Hafthor Björnsson: 501 kg (2020), 505 kg (July 2025), 510 kg (Sept 2025)—and won full contests while doing it.

Conclusion: Two Legacies

Eddie proved the “impossible” could be done. Thor took it further—again and again—and made the record his own.

Direct Comparison

Eddie Hall
  • First man to 500 kg—in competition—against all odds.
  • Sacrificed his body for the lift; passing out with a brain bleed.
Hafthor Björnsson
  • Took the record beyond: 501, 505, and 510 kg.
  • Made record lifts look smooth and still won same-day contests.
